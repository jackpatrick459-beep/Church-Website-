Our Full History

A journey of faith from the shores of Lake Victoria to the America District.

1903 – 1906

The Arrival at Gendia

The first Adventist missionaries to Kenya, Arthur Carscallen and Peter Nyambo, found much of the territory already "taken up" by other Christian missions. Navigating via Tanganyika, they landed at the port of Tanga and trekked to the Pare Mountains, where the first mission was established in 1903.

Escorted by German missionary A.C. Enn, they eventually reached Gendia, Kendu Bay, on the shores of Lake Victoria in 1906. Their immediate task was the establishment of a mission station that would serve as the cradle of Adventism in the region.

1909 – 1913

Expansion & Education

Education became the primary strategy for reaching the local community. A mission station was opened at Wire in 1909. Following the first baptisms at Gendia in 1911, the missionaries expanded their reach, establishing stations between 1912 and 1913 in Karungu, Kanyidoto, Kamagambo, Rusinga, and Nyanchwa in Gusii land.

The Pioneers

Dani Leah Onduru Oteko

Among the very first converts at Gendia was Dani Leah Onduru Oteko. Devotion in those early days required immense sacrifice; Leah and her fellow believers would carry their own food (nyoyo) every Friday evening, traveling to Kakwajwok, Konyango Majieri, and Kanyadhiang' just to arrive in time for the Sabbath.

Historical Note: Leah Onduru Oteko, wife to Jaduong Oteko Alwala, hailed from Kabar in Kisumu County. Her lineage is deeply intertwined with the region's history, including her sisters who married into families in Kanjira, and our grandmother, Margret Apiyo Amisi Oteko.

Leah was a true pioneer. When the journey to Gendia became too taxing for local converts, she championed the idea of a "Sabbath Branch." This request was granted on a trial basis, provided the locals took full responsibility for the church structures.

The Path to America Church

The Legacy of Kanyamfwa

The mission faced challenges, including an unwelcoming community at Oindo where Petro Adara Frontend Oindo initially hosted Sabbaths. Thanks to the intervention of Jakobo Olwa, the mission shifted to Kanyamfwa, where the community offered overwhelming support.

Over the years, the seeds planted by Leah and elders like Hezron Otondo and David Obuya blossomed. Leah’s influence even reached Gusii land at Nyanchwa, where she helped interpret for missionaries.

1975 – 2025

Modern Growth & America District

Today, we celebrate 50 years of the America SDA Church. What began as a Sabbath School from Nyamila SDA Church has matured into a District Headquarters overseeing six churches: Kuja, Alara Central, Kadimo, Oyokre, and Ajigo SDA.

As we look toward the future, we honor the "Gospel Champions" whose tireless efforts brought us from Gendia to Kanyamfwa, and finally to the establishment of the America District—now a center of excellence for education and health.
